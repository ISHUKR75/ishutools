Presets Guide

Which Preset Should You Choose?

Each preset is designed for a specific use case. Here's how to pick the right one.

🔮

Lossless

300 DPI · No JPEG re-encoding · pikepdf only

Zero image quality loss. Uses only pikepdf stream recompression. Images are mathematically identical after compression. Expected reduction: 2–25%.

✅ Best for: Legal documents, archival, print production, signed PDFs
💎

High Quality

200 DPI · JPEG q=82 · Stream recompression

Near-lossless quality. Stream recompression only — no DPI downsampling. Images look visually identical. Expected reduction: 10–40%.

✅ Best for: Presentations, portfolios, professional sharing, printing
⚖️

Medium

150 DPI · JPEG q=65 · Balanced

The recommended default for most users. Mild DPI reduction, good image quality. Expected reduction: 40–65%. Best balance of size vs quality.

✅ Best for: Most documents, online sharing, cloud storage
📧

Low

96 DPI · JPEG q=42 · Email-friendly

Designed for email attachments and messaging. Visible quality reduction in images but text remains crisp. Expected reduction: 55–78%.

✅ Best for: Email attachments, WhatsApp, Telegram, messaging apps
🔥

Screen

72 DPI · JPEG q=25 · Maximum compression

Maximum possible size reduction. Aggressive image quality reduction. Excellent for web/screen viewing. Expected reduction: 75–92%.

✅ Best for: Web viewing only, thumbnails, very slow internet upload
