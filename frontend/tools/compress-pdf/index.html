Presets Guide

Which Preset Should You Choose?

Each preset is optimised for a different use case. Here's exactly how to choose.

🔮

Lossless

300 DPI · No re-encoding · pikepdf only

Zero image quality loss. Uses only pikepdf stream recompression. Images are mathematically identical after compression. Expected reduction: 2–25%.

✅ Best for: Legal, archival, print, signed PDFs, court documents
💎

High Quality

200 DPI · JPEG q=82 · Near-lossless

Near-lossless quality. Stream recompression with mild image optimisation. Images look visually identical. Expected reduction: 10–40%.

✅ Best for: Presentations, portfolios, professional sharing, printing
⚖️

Medium

150 DPI · JPEG q=65 · Balanced

The recommended default for most users. Mild DPI reduction, good image quality. Expected reduction: 40–65%. Best balance of size vs quality for everyday use.

✅ Best for: Most documents, online sharing, cloud storage, Google Drive
📧

Low

96 DPI · JPEG q=42 · Email-friendly

Designed for email attachments and messaging. Visible quality reduction in images but text remains perfectly crisp. Expected reduction: 55–78%.

✅ Best for: Email, WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack, messaging apps
🔥

Screen

72 DPI · JPEG q=25 · Maximum

Maximum possible size reduction. Aggressive image quality reduction. Excellent for web/screen viewing only. Expected reduction: 75–92%.

✅ Best for: Web viewing only, thumbnails, very slow internet connections
