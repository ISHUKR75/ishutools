Preset Guide

Which Preset Should I Choose?

A guide to all 5 compression presets. IshuTools auto-recommends the best one for your file — but here's the full explanation.

🔮
Lossless
~2–25% reduction
Uses pikepdf only — zero image re-encoding. DEFLATE-9 stream recompression, ObjStm consolidation, cross-reference stream rebuild. Image data is mathematically identical (bit-for-bit) before and after.
Best for: Legal contracts, archival documents, technical drawings, CAD PDFs, medical scans, anything requiring 100% fidelity.
Engines: pikepdf, qpdf (stream only)
💎
High Quality
~10–40% reduction
Near-lossless compression with minimal image quality reduction. High JPEG quality (90+), conservative DPI, stream optimisation. Virtually indistinguishable from the original on screen or in print.
Best for: Professional reports, brochures, photo books, client presentations, anything where quality is important but some size reduction is needed.
Engines: pikepdf, gs_prepress, fitz high-DPI
⚖️
Medium ★ Recommended
~40–65% reduction
The recommended preset for most users. Balanced quality and compression. JPEG quality 75-85, moderate DPI downsampling, stream optimisation, font subsetting, metadata clean. Good quality on screen, acceptable for standard print.
Best for: General documents, homework, resumes, ebooks, forms, any everyday use where you want a noticeably smaller file without visible quality loss.
Engines: gs_ebook, fitz_medium, chain passes
📧
Low / Email
~55–78% reduction
Aggressive compression optimised for email attachments and messaging apps. JPEG quality 60-75, significant DPI reduction, metadata stripped, thumbnails removed, ICC profiles removed.
Best for: Email attachments under 5 MB limits, WhatsApp/Telegram sharing, government portal uploads, cloud storage space saving, quick sharing.
Engines: gs_screen, fitz_low, Pillow JPEG
🔥
Screen / Max
~75–92% reduction
Maximum possible compression. Very aggressive JPEG quality (40-60), heavy DPI downsampling (72 DPI), all metadata stripped, fonts subsetted, transparency flattened, grayscale conversion available. Best result size but lowest image quality.
Best for: Web display only, online portfolios, large scan archives, when storage space is critical and print quality is not required.
Engines: gs_screen, fitz_screen, pillow_webp, chains
Compression Technology

12 Engines, One Winner

All 12 engines run on your file in parallel. The smallest valid output that meets your quality settings is automatically returned.

🔷
pikepdf
DEFLATE-9 stream recompression, ObjStm consolidation, XRef stream rebuild. Zero image pixel re-encoding. Python's gold standard for lossless PDF optimisation.
Lossless
👻
Ghostscript (gs)
Industry-standard PDF distiller since 1988. screen / ebook / printer / prepress distil settings. Decades of production use in print industry. Very reliable for image-heavy PDFs.
Lossy
🦊
PyMuPDF (fitz)
Per-image DPI downsampling + JPEG/WebP re-encoding using the MuPDF library. Precise DPI control per preset (300/200/150/96/72 DPI). Excellent quality-to-size ratio.
Lossy
⚡
qpdf
Linearize + recompress streams. Lightning fast structural optimisation. Repairs cross-reference tables, recompresses Flate streams, removes redundant objects. No image quality change.
Stream
🐍
pypdf
compress_content_streams + orphan object purge. Pure Python fallback. Reliable for any PDF version. Removes unused and dead objects from the PDF object table.
Stream
🖼️
Pillow Advanced
JPEG progressive encoding + WebP lossless/lossy fallback. Extracts images, re-encodes them, and reinserts them into the PDF structure. Best results for image-heavy scan documents.
Lossy
🔧
mutool clean
MuPDF garbage collection + stream compression. Removes dead objects, recompresses existing streams, rebuilds cross-reference table. Fast and very reliable.
Stream
🔍
Deduplication
MD5-hash identical image streams across all pages — replaces duplicates with single-object references. Zero quality loss. Massive savings for multi-page reports with repeated logos/images.
Lossless
📊
Content Stream Opt
DEFLATE-9 recompression of PDF drawing commands (content streams). Whitespace removal, operator consolidation, colour operator optimisation. No visual change.
Stream
⛓️
GS + pikepdf Chain
Double-pass: Ghostscript distill first, then pikepdf lossless recompress the output. Combines lossy image reduction with lossless stream optimisation for maximum total savings.
Lossy
🔗
GS + fitz Chain
Ghostscript distill followed by PyMuPDF image polish pass. Best for image-heavy PDFs requiring two compression stages. Combines GS's distillation strength with fitz's per-image control.
Lossy
🎯
PyMuPDF Solo
Standalone fitz image pipeline without Ghostscript dependency. Faster processing when GS is unavailable. Excellent compression ratio for scan-based PDFs. Reliable fallback engine.
Lossy

Use Cases

When to Compress a PDF

Common real-world scenarios where compressing a PDF with IshuTools saves time and headaches.

Email Attachments

Gmail has a 25 MB attachment limit. Yahoo and Outlook have similar limits. Compress your PDF to meet these requirements using the Low preset or Target File Size mode. Quick presets: 5 MB, 2 MB, 1 MB.

Recommended: Low or Target Size

Government Portals

Indian government portals (DigiLocker, UIDAI, MCA, ITR filing) often require PDFs under 200 KB or 500 KB. Use Target File Size mode to hit exactly 200 KB or 500 KB every time.

Recommended: Target Size (200 KB)

College Applications

University online admission portals often have strict PDF size limits (2 MB, 5 MB). Compress your mark sheet, certificates, and documents while maintaining legibility with the Medium preset.

Recommended: Medium

Legal & Archival

Legal contracts, court filings, and archival documents must have zero quality loss. The Lossless preset uses pikepdf-only compression — image data is mathematically identical, only the PDF structure is optimised.

Recommended: Lossless

Website Performance

PDFs embedded in websites or served as downloads should be as small as possible for fast loading. Use Screen or Low preset for web-only PDFs. Linearize option enables fast first-page-view in browsers.

Recommended: Screen + Linearize

Professional Print

Brochures and reports sent to print shops need to be as small as possible for emailing while maintaining print quality. High preset preserves 300 DPI for professional printing with significant size reduction.

Recommended: High or Lossless
