What is PDF Splitting? — Explained by Ishu Kumar

PDF splitting (also called PDF extraction or PDF bursting) is the process of dividing a single PDF file into multiple smaller PDF files. Ishu Kumar built IshuTools Split PDF to make this process completely free, instant, and lossless for everyone.

When you split a PDF, you might want to: extract specific pages, divide chapters, create email-friendly parts, or separate scanned forms. Ishu Kumar designed 8 dedicated modes to handle every scenario — no other free tool comes close.

Ishu Kumar's 8 Split Modes — Explained

  • All Pages (Burst): Every page becomes its own PDF. Perfect for splitting scanned documents into individual pages.
  • Page Range: Extract any pages you need. "2-5, 8, 11-15" gives you exactly those pages in one PDF.
  • Range Groups (Exclusive — Ishu Kumar): Each range becomes its own separate PDF in one operation. Unique to IshuTools — no other free tool has this.
  • Every N Pages: Split into equal chunks. "Every 5 pages" creates part1.pdf (1-5), part2.pdf (6-10), etc.
  • By Bookmarks/Chapters: Each chapter becomes its own named PDF. Ideal for textbooks and manuals.
  • Blank Separator: Automatically detects blank pages used as dividers in scanned form stacks. Ishu Kumar's proprietary detection algorithm.
  • By File Size: Split until each part is under your chosen MB limit. Perfect for email size restrictions.
  • Odd/Even Pages: Two files — odd pages and even pages. Perfect for duplex-scanned documents.

Why Zero Quality Loss? — Ishu Kumar's Technical Choice

Ishu Kumar made a deliberate technical decision when building IshuTools: use pikepdf with recompress_flate=False. This means every image, font, and embedded object inside your PDF is byte-copied — not decoded, not re-encoded, just copied exactly as-is.

Other free tools often run PDFs through Ghostscript's re-encoding pipeline, which degrades image quality imperceptibly at first but visibly over repeated operations. Ishu Kumar's approach gives you bit-for-bit identical quality — verified with post-write page count checks in every split operation.
